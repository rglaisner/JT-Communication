Jeremy Trainer V1 – Live conversational AI

Phase 1 jeremy-trainer: a conversational trainer (text-first + audio analysis) that saves sessions, extracts traits, and connects into persona and content-draft generation. Historical and live social posts will feed the same schema so the Digital Twin stays aligned with Jeremy’s public voice.

Goal
  • Jeremy can run real sessions today; sessions are persisted with traits extraction.
  • Clear path from trainer → console (sessions, persona, drafts).
  • Schema and pipeline design for past/live social media → persona and content-draft pipeline.
Phase 1 scope (current)
  • Trainer → console API: All calls use console backend; Zod-validated payloads; transcript-based flow (no live connect yet).
  • Session flow: Session setup, structured transcript builder (turns), audio helper for voice match only.
  • Preflight: Status panel checks Supabase, JEREMY_USER_ID, Gemini key.
  • Console links: Deep link to session page; persona update path; content-draft from session (Phase 1.5).
  • Guardrails: Copy explaining sessions; validation (no empty user turns); graceful errors.
Social integration (Phases 2–3)
  • Phase 2: Historical social import (e.g. social_posts table), X/LinkedIn ingestion → memories and story fragments; console review UI.
  • Phase 3: Live webhooks/polling; “Persona vs Live” dashboard; consistency checks and suggestions for Strategist/Muse.

What we’re building
For Jeremy · Plain English

The Digital Twin is an always‑on partner that watches how you talk, what you care about, and how you like to show up online. It takes your rawest inputs — a voice note, a video clip, a half‑written idea — and turns them into post‑ready drafts that still sound like you.

You stay in control: at first, every post is reviewed and edited by you. Over time, as you trust it, the twin can start handling simpler, low‑risk posts on its own, always within clear guardrails.

How it works under the hood
Tech stack · Human language

Website & app. A modern web app (Next.js + React) acts as your control room: chat with the twin, review suggestions, and approve posts.

Brain & memory. A Postgres database (Supabase) stores your conversations, preferences, and “memories” — things the system should remember about you as an artist, a dad, and a person.

AI helpers. Google’s Gemini models handle conversation and understanding your voice and content; other models help with planning and analytics. Your raw data stays in our database, not in the model providers.

Data & privacy
Where your data lives

Everything you share with the twin — voice memos, transcripts, drafts — is stored in a private database just for this project. The AI models see only what they need for each task, and providers are configured so they don’t use your data to train their own systems.

You’ll always know which parts of your history are “in play” when the system generates suggestions, and we can turn specific data sources on or off as you like.

First deliverable: Voice‑learning app
Hands‑on for Jeremy

The first thing you’ll use is a dedicated voice‑learning app. You’ll drop in for short sessions where the AI leads the conversation and listens for:

  • How you talk about your story, fatherhood, and music.
  • The slang you naturally use (and what feels corny).
  • Your natural pacing, tone, and emotional baseline.

Each session updates an internal “Jeremy profile” that every other part of the system uses before suggesting content.

For the first week, we’ll follow a simple three‑session script to “feed the machine”:

  • Session 1 – who you are and why you make music.
  • Session 2 – a live show recap and how you sound on stage.
  • Session 3 – boundaries: what’s off‑limits and what feels corny or too much.

After each session, the trainer links you into the console so you can inspect the saved transcript, see the voice traits snapshot, and peek at the Persona page to watch your digital twin’s profile evolve in real time.
